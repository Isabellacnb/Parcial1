Tribute to Katherine Johnson

“Like what you do, and then you will do your best."
- Katherine, Interview at Nasa, 1966.

TIMELINE

  • Aug 23, 1939 Katherine was selected to be one of the first African American students to enroll at West Virginia University at Morgantown.
  • Sep 12, 1953 She began work as a computer at NASA.
  • Oct 09, 1960 Johnson co authored a paper on calculations for placing a spacecraft into orbit.
  • Dec 13, 1961 Katherine calculated the path for Freedom 7.
  • Aug 10, 1962 John Glenn became the first American to orbit Earth. By that time, NASA had begun to use electronic computers. However, before Glenn left the ground, he wanted to make sure that the electronic computer had planned his flight correctly. He asked to have Johnson double check the computer’s calculations.
  • Jan 22, 1969 Katherine was also part of the team that calculated where and when to launch the rocket for the Apollo 11 mission of 1969, which sent the first three men to the Moon.
  • Feb 16, 2015 She was awarded the U.S Presidential Medal of Freedom
